Writing git commit messages is an important and incredibly difficult aspect of programming. After making a large amount of changes, I don't always want to go back over everything I've reviewed. Naturally IDEs allow you to generate messages inline but even this is not perfect.

## A good commit?

Good commit messages require context you lose the moment you move on to the next thing. You know why you made the change, the diff knows what changed, but translating that into a message that makes sense to someone else - or to you in three weeks - takes effort.
